01 — The brief

Can an AI-run finance function — with a licensed human at the judgment layer — be a viable and defensible business for Indonesia's mid-market?

This final project develops and tests a business plan for a service that gives a mid-market firm a finance department without the cost of building one. AI reads the firm's accounting data and answers everyday finance questions; a licensed accountant holds the judgment and the liability. The work is assessed on three fronts — a real market that can pay, a real pain worth solving, and a legal path to operate.

02 — The problem

Mid-market firms have outgrown the spreadsheet — but can't justify a CFO.

03 — The concept

What is offered.

01

It does the work

Connects to the firm's existing accounting system (e.g., Mekari Jurnal) and answers finance questions — cash, profit, receivables — in plain language.

02

It earns trust

Every figure is traceable to its source; a licensed human reviews the work and owns the decision. Designed against hallucination, not around it.

03

The constraint is the moat

Serving accounting to the public legally requires a licensed entity (Kantor Jasa Akuntan). The mandatory human layer is both compliance and a barrier to software-only competitors.

04 — Strategic approach

Design Thinking, applied with the tools of strategic analysis.

Design Thinking

A user-led, evidence-first method — validate the problem and the demand before committing to build.

Strategic frameworks

PESTEL (regulatory axis first), Porter's Five Forces, VRIO, and the Business Model Canvas.

Primary validation

Interviews with mid-market owners, finance leaders, and licensed accountants to test size, willingness-to-pay, and partnership economics.

Empathise→Define→Ideate→Prototype→Test
